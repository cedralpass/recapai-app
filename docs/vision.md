# Vision for Recap AI

As a busy person, I just don't have time to read every LinkedIn post, blog article, or other media I find interesting. I may scan it, but then lose it. Recap solves that.

Recap is your personal reading memory. Bookmark anything and Recap will save it, summarize it, categorize it, and build your own knowledge taxonomy so you can find it later. Every evening it sends your reading list, and every weekend it delivers a newspaper-style recap for your morning coffee.

Your reading history is private by default — your knowledge, your data.

# Style

Recap is designed to fire off a bookmark from the browser with our Chrome extension or simply by pasting into an entry box. The design is minimal so you can focus on reading and your knowledge. It should be easy to scroll through article summaries and click off to them for reading.

# Current Features

- Login and create account
- Save article from web form and Chrome extension
- Browse and filter articles by personal taxonomy
- Organize taxonomy automatically leveraging AI (in progress)

# Planned Ideas

## Content Ingestion
- Add an iPhone / iPad share extension to save articles from your mobile device
- Pull newsletters from Gmail and extract their articles into your memory
- Expand to podcasts and videos, pulling transcripts into memory

## Taxonomy & Organization
- Improve taxonomy by adding article metadata and summary context to AI classification
- Guide the AI with personal preferences, e.g. "Always merge cooking and food tips into Gastronomy"
- Add read/unread state to articles so you can track what you have actually read and clean up your list

## Digests
- Daily digest sent at the time you wrap up your day
- Weekly digest sent on the weekend when you want to review the week

## Social & Sharing
- Add friends and family so you can share your taxonomy and discoveries
- Secure private items so they stay visible only to you
